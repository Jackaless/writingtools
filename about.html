What is ProseCraft.net?

ProseCraft.net is a free suite of text analysis tools designed for writers, editors, educators, students, and anyone who cares about communicating clearly. Every tool on this site is free to use, requires no account or sign-up, and works entirely in your browser — your text is never sent to any server.

The tools are built around a simple belief: good writing is clear writing. Whether you're checking the readability of a student essay, eliminating passive voice from a business report, or estimating the reading time of a newsletter, these tools give you the data to make informed decisions about your writing.

Our tools

  • Readability CheckerAnalyses text for Flesch Reading Ease score, Flesch-Kincaid grade level, reading time, and key writing statistics.
  • Passive Voice DetectorHighlights every passive voice construction in your text and gives you an active voice score out of 100.
  • Word CounterCounts words, characters, sentences, paragraphs, unique words, reading time, speaking time, and estimated pages.
  • Reading Time CalculatorCalculates reading and speaking time at adjustable speeds, with comparisons to everyday time benchmarks.

Who uses these tools?

Teachers and educators use our readability and passive voice tools to give students concrete, objective feedback on their writing — going beyond "this is unclear" to show exactly where and why.

Content writers and marketers use the readability checker and word counter to hit the right length and reading level for their audience before publishing.

Academic writers use the passive voice detector to balance active and passive constructions in essays, dissertations, and research papers.

Publishers and editors use the suite as a quick first-pass quality check before deeper editing begins.

Privacy and your data

All analysis happens entirely in your browser. No text you enter is ever transmitted to our servers, stored, or seen by anyone. You can safely paste confidential documents, unpublished manuscripts, or student work without any privacy concern.
